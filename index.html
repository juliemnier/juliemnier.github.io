About me


I'm a third year PhD Student in physical oceanography at Paris-Saclay University and SPEC lab, CEA Saclay. I am working under the supervision of Basile Gallet on ocean mesoscale turbulence and the parametrization of the associated tracer transport.

My main research interests cover understanding ocean dynamics, specifically mesoscale and submesoscale processes, but also 2D
and quasi-2D turbulent flows and vortex dynamics.

I am also teaching assistant at ENSTA Paris in Compressible Fluid Dynamics at 3rd year Bachelor level and Turbulence Theory at 1st year Master level. 

Research topics

I mainly work on parametrizing unresolved ocean mesoscale transport for 1Â° resolution ocean general circulation models that use the Gent-McWilliams/Redi parametrization (first panel). To build a physically-based parametrization of mesoscale turbulence, I study transport by the baroclinic instability in 3D direct numerical simulations (DNS) of an idealized patch of ocean (second panel), using the pseudo-spectral solver CORAL by Benjamin Miquel. I also study the dynamics of coherent vortices in two-dimensional and quasi-two-dimensional turbulent flows, and their impact on the associated inverse energy cascade and their transport properties (see third panel for simulation of the inverse cascade of 2D Navier-Stokes). At some point I also took an interest in the beta-effect and zonal flows transport properties that I investigated with 3D DNS (not shown here).
